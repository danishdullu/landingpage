FAQs?

How does a Protected Portfolio work?

A Protected Portfolio is an FDIC insured bank deposit combined with market performance. The bank deposit is a CD provided by your bank while the market performance is driven by an advanced portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

How do I get market returns?

Traditional bank CDs have two components: i) your bank deposit ii) the fixed interest that the bank pays you on your deposit.

Figure 1: Traditional bank deposits return a fixed interest

At the end of a traditional CD term, you receive the deposit and any interest earned. A Protected Portfolio has a similar structure but with a key difference: here the CD’s interest is invested in the markets instead. As a result, Protected Portfolio ™ components are: i) your bank deposit ii) market return.

Figure 2: Protected Portfolio invests your bank deposit interest in the markets.

At the end of a Protected Portfolio term, you receive the deposit and any market performance earned.

What is a reasonable expectation of Protected Portfolio return?

The return presented is the average return over a given period. This means that at times investors will earn more than the average return while at other times they will earn less. Never, however, will an investor earn less than zero.

What happens if the portfolio declines below zero? Who bears that risk?

The portfolios are delivered by a portfolio certificate issued by a highly rated financial institution. This means that the financial institution makes the actual investment not you. A portfolio certificate can decline in value to $0, but it cannot decline in value beyond $0.

How is it 100% principal protected?

The invested amount purchases an FDIC insured deposit. Only the interest is invested in the market linked portfolio.

How is it FDIC insured?

Bank deposits under $250,000 are FDIC insured. Protected Portfolio’s bank deposit is no different.
